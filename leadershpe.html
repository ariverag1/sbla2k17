Meet the LeaderSHPE

Faviola Loera - President



Piping Stress Engineer
ISAT Seismic Bracing
Email: favloera@gmail.com

Faviola graduated from the University of California, San Diego (UCSD) with a Bachelors of Science in Mechanical Engineering in June 2015. She began her professional career in the summer of 2012 at Technip Stone & Webster Process Technology as a Piping Stress Engineer Intern and returned for two additional summer internships. She is now working as a Piping Stress Engineer at ISAT Seismic Bracing.

Faviola got involved with the SHPE UCSD chapter during her sophomore year in college and after attending the Regional Leadership Development Conference (RLDC) in Arizona, she quickly became an active member. She then decided to take on the role of RLDC 2014 Chair where she organized a two-day event that promoted professional development and leadership enhancement for more than 300 SHPE students and professionals. Faviola was also involved with RLDC 2015 as the Social Media Chair. She has been the SHPE Region 2 Summer Leadership Retreat (SLR) Chair for years 2014 and 2015. In her free time, Faviola enjoys CrossFit, running, hiking, traveling, and coffee!



Anthony Becerril - Internal Vice-President



Electrical Engineering, B.S.
Cal State Long Beach, Recent Graduate
Email: apbecerril@gmail.com

Anthony is a recent graduate from California State University, Long Beach and received a Bachelors of Science in Electrical Engineering. Apart from school, he has had the pleasure of gaining summer experiences 2013-2016 for University of California, Berkeley's Center for Energy Efficient Electronics Science (UCB E3S), Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) [2 summers], and Honda R&D Americas Inc. respectively. All this experiences has helped him grow professionally and maintain a competitive edge in industry.

As for SHPE, Anthony has taken on various roles throughout his years. From 2012-2016 he has take on Chapter Event Chair, Chapter Co-President, Vice Regional Student Representative, Regional Student Representative respectively. In these roles he has gained vast experience about teamwork, leadership, networking, and much more. He plans to apply these skills to his new role and help give back to the community to make it grow in success, confidence, and pride.

Before all this, he was simply a kid growing up in the streets of North Long Beach. Now he has made success in his dreams of STEM and continues to grow by giving back, continually learning, and showing that it is possible to make success of and from anything and anywhere.




Brian Vazquez - External Vice-President



Adjunct Faculty
East Los Angeles College
Email: vazqueb@gmail.com

Brian Vazquez is a proud product of the community college system and after struggling to balance school and work he excelled at East Los Angeles College (ELAC). He obtained his Associates of Arts degree along with a Computer Aided Drafting Certificate while preparing to transfer to a four-year institution. During this same period he worked for NGV Ecotrans Group LLC converting vehicles to run on Compressed Natural Gas. This busy schedule did not detour him from being active on campus with the Engineering Club and the Society Hispanic Professional Engineers.

Mr. Vazquez later obtained his Mechanical Engineering degree from California State University, Los Angeles (CSULA) where he continued to be an active student leader of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers and served as the Human Powered Vehicle design team captain. During this educational period he worked full-time as the Engineering Laboratory Technician at ELAC.

He continues to balance school and work as a part-time engineering and technology instructor at ELAC and Pierce College as well as a Teaching Associate while studying for his Masters in Mechanical Engineering at CSULA. Lastly, he serves as a mentor to engineering students and is active in the STEM community motivating individuals to become practicing engineers while creating a legacy that future generations may look up to as role models.

Manuel Carrasco - Treasurer



System Engineering
Northrop Grumman
Email: carrasco.manny@gmail.com

Manuel graduated in 2007 from California State Polytechnic, University of Pomona with a Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering. After a few years of working as a System Test Engineer at Northrop Grumman he returned to Cal Poly, Pomona for a Masters in Electrical Engineering with an emphasis in Analog and Digital Communications. Manuel is currently pursuing an MBA from the University of Pepperdine with an emphasis in Finance. With over 8 years of experience in the Aerospace industry, he has worked on various programs in both Space and Air. He has also studied in areas of manufacturing and entrepreneurship.

Manuel has previous treasurer experience with the Space Park Toastmasters Chapter. In his spare time he volunteers for the Big Brother Big Sisters of America Organization. He has experience presenting to college and high school students about college and his experience in STEM. He first became involved with SHPE during his undergraduate studies and believed in the work SHPE does for students. He hopes to develop himself as well as others professionally during his role as treasurer.





Cathalina Juarez - Director of IT



Program Specialist
USC Viterbi School of Engineering
Email: cathalina@outlook.com

Cathalina Juarez is a program specialist at USC's Viterbi School of Engineering in the Engineering Education division. In her spare time, she volunteers with the Latinas in STEM Foundation, the SHPE South Bay Los Angeles Professional Chapter, and SpaceHorizons. Ms. Juarez was raised in Maryland. Her parents immigrated to the United States and settled in Maryland in order to provide their children with the best possible educational opportunities. She says her natural curiosity, since childhood, ultimately led her to science. In high school, her science experiment won awards at the county science fair, including a scholarship from Sigma Xi, The Scientific Research Society. She went on to receive two bachelor’s degrees from the University of Maryland, Baltimore County – a B.S. in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, and a B. A. in Psychology. She went on to receive a master’s degree in Education from California State University, Long Beach.
